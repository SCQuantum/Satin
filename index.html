MOON

Fact: 1

It is a dusty ball of rock which is home to several mountains, craters, and seas made of hardened lava

Fact: 2

This is Earth’s only natural satellite as it orbits in form of an ellipse around the globe every 27.3 days

Fact: 3

Every year the Moon drifts an approximate 3.8 cm away from the Earth. This means that in about 50 billion years, the Moon will take 47 days to orbit rather than 27.3.

Fact: 4

There are slight moonquakes on the moon caused by the gravitational pull on Earth. This was measured when astronauts took seismographs to the moon to measure the quakes.

Fact: 5

The Moon controls the tides of the Earth through the gravitational pull.

RESOURCE

Fact: 1

The first moon landing took place in 1969 when Neil Armstrong, Edwin “Buzz” Aldrin, and Michael Collin landed.

Fact: 2

The race to the moon was against the Soviet Union. Kennedy boldly proposed to send men to the moon and the Cold-War era America accepted this. By 1966, the first unmanned test took place to test the structure of the spacecraft.

Fact: 3

After a failed test during 1967, America still had yet to lose hope. In 1968, a successful orbit around Earth was conducted, testing several systems needed for the moon landing.

Fact: 4

Finally, on July 20 1969, after a 76 hour travel from the Earth to the moon, the spacecraft landed.
